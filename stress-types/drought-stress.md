# Drought Stress Physiology

## Purpose

This file summarizes drought stress from a plant stress physiology perspective, including the primary limitation, major mechanisms, plant responses, measurable traits, phenotyping tools, and biological interpretation.

Drought stress is not only a lack of water. It is a whole-plant physiological condition where soil water deficit, atmospheric demand, hydraulic limitation, stomatal regulation, carbon balance, root architecture, hormonal signaling, oxidative stress, and reproductive sensitivity interact to determine growth, survival, yield, and recovery.

---

# 1. Definition

Drought stress occurs when water availability is insufficient to meet plant water demand. It may result from low soil moisture, high evaporative demand, high vapor pressure deficit, restricted root uptake, poor soil hydraulic conductivity, or a combination of these factors.

In crop physiology, drought should be interpreted as a dynamic stress that changes over time and depends on:

- Soil water availability
- Root distribution
- Crop growth stage
- Atmospheric demand
- Vapor pressure deficit
- Canopy size
- Genotype
- Management system
- Stress duration
- Recovery conditions

---

# 2. Primary physiological limitation

The central limitation under drought is reduced plant water status.

```text
Low soil water availability
        ↓
Reduced root water uptake
        ↓
Declining plant water potential
        ↓
Loss of cell turgor
        ↓
Reduced cell expansion, stomatal closure, and metabolic limitation
        ↓
Lower growth, yield, and quality
