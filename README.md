## Purpose

This repository is a structured knowledge hub for plant stress physiology research. It organizes abiotic stress mechanisms, physiological limitations, plant responses, measurable traits, phenotyping tools, experimental design principles, and data interpretation frameworks.

The goal is to build a practical information center for crop stress physiology, sustainable horticulture, phenotyping, and climate-resilient crop production research.

## Scientific foundation

This repository is inspired by foundational concepts  related to water relations, photosynthesis, stomatal biology, mineral nutrition, respiration, signal transduction, senescence, and abiotic stress.

All content in this repository is written for educational, research, and portfolio purposes. Textbook figures, tables, and copied passages should not be uploaded here. 
## Main abiotic stress categories

- Drought stress
- Salinity stress
- Heat stress
- Chilling and freezing stress
- Flooding and waterlogging stress
- Light and oxidative stress
- Nutrient stress
- Heavy metal stress
- Ozone and ultraviolet stress
- Combined abiotic stresses

## Core stress physiology framework

```text
Abiotic stress exposure
        ↓
Stress perception
        ↓
Signal transduction
        ↓
Physiological adjustment
        ↓
Biochemical and hormonal response
        ↓
Growth and developmental trade-offs
        ↓
Yield, quality, survival, and recovery outcomes
